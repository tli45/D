Improving Your Ollie

Learn how to jump higher, farther, and with more control.

How to Jump Higher

  • Strong Pop: Slam the tail down hard with your back foot.
  • Quick Front Foot Slide: The faster and sharper the slide, the more lift you get.
  • Knees to Chest: Tuck your knees up to allow more board clearance.
  • Commitment: Don’t bail mid-air — stay over the board!

How to Jump Farther

  • Speed Matters: Approach with a smooth, controllable speed.
  • Forward Momentum: Push your body forward during the pop to carry distance.
  • Level Pop: Try to keep the board flat in the air to maintain forward glide.

Exercises to Practice

  • Practice Ollies on grass or carpet for confidence.
  • Use chalk to mark where you land and measure distance gains.
  • Film yourself to review posture and timing.
  • Try Ollieing over small obstacles (sticks, bottles).
